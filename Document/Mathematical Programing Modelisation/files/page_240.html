12.9 Economic Planning
An economy consists of three industries: coal, steel and transport. Each unit produced by one of the industries (a unit will be taken as £1's worth of value of production) requires inputs from possibly its own industry as well as other industries. The required inputs as well as the manpower requirements (also measured in £) are given in Table 12.1. There is a time lag in the economy so that output in year t + 1 requires an input in year t.
Output from an industry may also be used to build productive capacity for itself or other industries in future years.
