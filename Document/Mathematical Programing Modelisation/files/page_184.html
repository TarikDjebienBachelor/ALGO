Some of these different formulations are given by Miller, Tucker and Zemlin (1960), Gavish and Graves (1978), Claus (1984), Fox, Gavish and Graves (1980) and Finke, Claus and Gunn (1983). The formulation above is due to Dantzig, Fulkerson and Johnson (1954).
There are also variants and extensions of the Travelling Salesman problem. One such is the MILK COLLECTION problem described in Part 2. This is based on a paper by Butler, Williams and Yarrow (1997).
A comprehensive survey of the problem giving a number of alternative formulations is Lawler et al. (1995). Eilon, Watson-Gandy and Christofides (1971) describe how the vehicle scheduling problem can be reduced to a travelling salesman problem.
Special algorithms have been developed for the travelling salesman problem. One of the most successful is that of Held and Karp (1971).
The Quadratic Assignment Problem
This is a generalization of the assignment problem described in Section 5.3. Whereas that problem could be treated as an LP problem and was therefore comparatively easy to solve, the quadratic assignment problem is a genuine IP problem and often very difficult to solve.
Like the assignment problem we will consider the problem as related to two sets of objects S and T. S and T both have the same number of members which will be indexed 1 to n.
