Some comment should be made concerning the blending of fuel oil where the ingredients (light, heavy, and cracked oil and residuum) are taken in fixed proportions. Here it might be preferable to think of the production of FO as an activity. It is common in the oil industry to think in terms of activities rather than quantities. In Section 3.4 modal formulations are discussed where activities represent the extreme modes of operation of a process. Here we have a special case of this of a process with one mode of operation. The level of this activity then fixes the proportions of the ingredients, in a case such as this, automatically.
