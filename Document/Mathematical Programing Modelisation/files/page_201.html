Using the LP formulation described for the 01 example in Part 2 it would be necessary to impose conditions such as (7) as extra LP constraints for the general integer case.
The effort of trying to simplify single integer constraints in this way will often not be worthwhile. In many models these constraints represent logical conditions and are already in their simplest form as single constraints. Applications where such simplification could possibly prove worthwhile are project selection and capital budgeting problems. It also proves worthwhile to simplify single constraints in this way in the MARKET SHARING problem presented in Part 2.
The simplification of a single 01 constraint into another single 01 constraint considered here has been described by Bradley, Hammer and Wolsey (1974).
